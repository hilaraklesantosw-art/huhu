# Autumn

Autumn has arrived, and the leaves have turned yellow.

The rice ripens, hanging its head as if resigned to fate. The apples hang red and purple from the branches, waiting to be plucked. The maple leaves are red, the ginkgo yellow—beautiful, yes, but what comes after beauty? Nothing but decay into mud.

The wild geese fly south, crying out, as if bidding farewell, or perhaps sighing. The Mid-Autumn moon is full, but people are not necessarily reunited.

They say autumn is the season of harvest. Yet after harvest comes withering. Gratitude? I rather think that in this world, most things must be repaid.
