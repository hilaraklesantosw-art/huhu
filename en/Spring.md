# Spring

Spring has come again.

The ice on the ground has turned to water, and the water has flowed into the ditches. Green buds emerge from the branches, timid and shy, as if afraid to face the world. The wind has grown warmer, yet when it blows across one's face, it still carries the earthy scent of soil.

The swallows have returned, chattering beneath the eaves. In the fields, farmers bend over, scattering seeds into the earth—they hope for autumn, but will autumn truly grant them their wishes?

I have always been willing to speculate about nature with the worst of malice, yet spring has arrived all the same. Hope or despair, life must go on.
