# Summer

Summer is parching.

The cicadas cry desperately in the trees, as though they would die if they ceased. The sunlight is poisonous, scorching everything. The shade beneath the trees offers relief, but such relief is only temporary. The lotus flowers in the pond bloom in full glory, yet how many truly stop to behold them?

Children play in the water; elders sit beneath the trees, fanning themselves with palm-leaf fans. At night, fireflies flicker on and off, like the last remaining light in this world.

Some say summer is passionate. I, however, find this passion far too intense—it leaves one gasping for air. When life is at its most vigorous, it is often also closest to decay.
