# Winter

Winter is silent.

The snow falls, covering everything. The earth is white, the trees are white, even the rooftops are white. This whiteness is pure, yet also cold and merciless. Icicles hang from the eaves, crystal clear, yet radiating cold.

People gather around the stove, drinking hot tea, speaking of trivial matters. Children play in the snow—there is laughter, yes, but how long can such laughter last?

Winter is a season of settling—I have never believed such pretty words. Winter is winter; cold is cold. There is nothing to embellish. As for waiting for spring? What if spring comes? It is merely another cycle.
